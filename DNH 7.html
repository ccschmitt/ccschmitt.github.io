DNH 7 (Eurydice)

DNH 7 is the archive number of almost completely preserved skull with associated mandible of Paranthropus robustus , in 1994 in the province of Gauteng , South Africa was discovered. In close proximity to this fossil , a further, very well preserved lower jaw (was DNH 8 ) discovered the the same kind belongs. On the basis of animal fossils from the same Fund layer the age of both individuals was estimated at 1.5 to 2 million years. DNH 7 is sometimes nicknamed "Eurydice" refers DNH 8 as " Orpheus ". The acronym DNH stands for Drimolen Homind , place of custody of fossils is the School of Anatomical Sciences of Witwatersrand University .
