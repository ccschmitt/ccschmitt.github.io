The Hominin Map Project

This is my final project from my Graphic Design in Cartography course. I wanted to make something that both relates to my background, and helps others learn about human evolution. For this project, I used Leaflet, which involved the use of html, javascript, and css. Each skull icon represents the approximate location of a hominin discovery. Click the skulls to learn more about the find, and follow the links to the newly created extension to my website! Here, I have created individual pages for most of the discoveries. If there wasn't too much information available for a find, I simply linked you to external sources which may be helpful.

To view the map, click here. To go directly to the Hominin site, click here.

Note: I do not own any of the material (images of skulls, written information, etc). I just tried to collect as much information as I could, given my timeframe, and put it all together. Brief introductions for fossils were copied from their Wiki pages, as were the images (though I photoshopped each image to remove backgrounds). I say again (so I don't get in trouble for plagiarism), I did not write any of the text on the pages that discuss discoveries, that information was taken from their respective Wikipedia pages, and there is a link directly below that information to where it came from. The Wiki information was checked for accuracy.

Again, No copyright infringement or plagiarism intended. I only had a few weeks to put this all together, so writing all of this information on my own wouldn't have been possible. Also note, project was not specifically designed for mobile use, so things may look wonky on mobile devices.
